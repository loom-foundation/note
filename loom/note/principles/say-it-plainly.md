Corpus prose says what it means in the plainest form that is still precise: short, direct sentences, one consistent voice, and no ornament a reader must decode.

Clarity is weighed against precision, not set above it: where a plain phrasing would drop a needed distinction, the distinction wins, but the default is the plainest wording that keeps it.

## Why this matters

The corpus is read cold, by a newcomer and by an AI agent operating on a subset of the files, and at machine speed an unclear sentence compounds: it is acted on wrongly, cited wrongly, and re-stated wrongly downstream.
Clarity is the craft the method exists to protect, the reason intent is written down at all rather than left in someone's head; prose that has to be decoded is intent that was not really captured.

The risk this principle mitigates is misread intent: a reader or agent acting on what the prose seemed to say rather than what it meant.

## How to apply

Prefer the short sentence, the common word, and the active voice; cut the phrase that adds rhythm but no meaning.
When a sentence needs an aside, reach for a comma, a colon, a semicolon, parentheses, or a second sentence, and hold one consistent spelling and register across the corpus, so a reader spends attention on the meaning and never on the variant.
Read a passage as a stranger would: if it has to be decoded, rewrite it.

## Considered alternative

Write in a richer register, with the asides and flourishes that make prose pleasant to a careful human reader.

Discarded because the corpus's readers include agents and hurried reviewers, and the cost of a misread at machine speed outweighs the pleasure of an ornamented sentence; a renderer can add warmth, but the source must read true on a cold pass.

## Origin

General English (Oxford, "plain": "easy to perceive or understand; clear", and "not elaborate or complicated").
The lineage is the plain-language tradition (the plain-English movement and the U.S. Plain Writing Act of 2010) and the technical-writing standard that values clarity, brevity, and consistency over style; in this corpus it is the working form of the manifesto's claim that when machines build at speed, clarity becomes the craft.
