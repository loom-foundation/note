A decision may carry additional level-2 sections after its required set.
The required sections are canonical and come first; an additional section is legal where it carries record the canonical set does not.

## Context

Five current decisions carry sections outside the canonical set: Residual risk, Frameworks surveyed (twice), Reopening conditions, and Panel findings and their resolutions.
The spine pack fixed the required decision sections but was silent on additional ones, so an author could not know whether the pattern was legal, and a checker had nothing to say but "undeclared".
The content itself is genuine record (survey evidence, deferral triggers, panel dispositions), earned under the same bar as everything else.

## Decision

- Additional level-2 sections on a decision are legal, after the required set.
- The required sections remain canonical, in their fixed order, and an additional section never substitutes for one of them.
- An additional section is held to Earned Substance like any other: it carries record the canonical set does not, or it is deleted in review.
- The spine pack's decision-kind section states this in one sentence.

## Forces and trade-offs

An open section set costs uniformity: a reader may meet a heading no template predicted.
Accepted: the alternative forces genuine record into canonical headings where nobody would look for it, and uniformity of the required set is preserved because additional sections come after it.
The Earned Substance bar, not a closed list, is what keeps ceremony out.

## Alternatives considered

- **Retitle the five sections into the canonical set.**
  Rejected: survey evidence buried in Forces and reopening triggers buried in the Decision are harder to find, and the next decision with genuine extra record faces the same wall.
- **Enumerate the extra sections as new optional canonical sections.**
  Rejected: a closed list grows by amendment forever, taxing every future decision for the shape of five past ones.
