A Reviewer or Auditor can point to the exact information the actor was given for a task, confirm it was current and approved when the task ran, and rule out any unreviewed text, so the review rests on a complete and verifiable record and a finding can be traced to its true cause rather than guessed at.

## Illustrative situations

When the record is complete, a Reviewer or Auditor who finds a policy violation can tell which of three causes produced it: the actor never held the policy, held a version that had since been superseded, or held the current version and departed from it anyway. The three are indistinguishable from the output alone yet each calls for a different response, and only a record of what the actor was actually given separates them.
