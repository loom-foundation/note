When an Adopter takes up the method, it wants the discipline to match the stakes of what it is building, so a prototype is not buried in process and a regulated system is not under-protected.

## Source

The widely documented failure of one-size-fits-all process: heavyweight methodologies abandoned by small teams as unearned ceremony, and light process proving inadequate at scale.

The motivating principle, proportionality, is itself settled in law and risk management, but the tier here grades the observed reality of the problem, not the status of the principle.
