An Adopter can express its intent in the kinds of work product it already uses, so adoption reads as recognition of what the Adopter already does rather than a demand to relearn a fixed kind set.
If the method presents a maximal, undifferentiated list of kinds, an Adopter whose domain already has a working vocabulary must either ignore the unfamiliar kinds or relearn its own vocabulary in the method's terms, and either path makes adoption feel like cost before any value is gained.

## Source

This is a reasoned hypothesis about adopters the method has not yet met.
It is grounded in the observation that methods with fixed maximal kind sets are resisted by teams with entrenched vocabularies, but the specific adopter population and their kind-systems are not yet observed.
Validation tier is therefore `inferred`.

## Illustrative situations

1. **Regulated systems adopter.**
   An adopter that already speaks in requirements, specifications, and acceptance tests expects those three kinds to map directly.
   A method that supplies them without asking the adopter to rename its artifacts reads as confirmation of existing practice.

2. **Product-discovery adopter.**
   An adopter that already uses jobs, pains, and gains as its working vocabulary does not want to retrain on an alien kind set;
   it wants to enable those kinds and work in them.

3. **Lean adopter with only needs and decisions.**
   A lean adopter capturing intent at low ceremony wants to turn on exactly the kinds it earns and ignore the rest;
   anything it must read past is ceremony the method is imposing without return.

4. **SAFe adopter.**
   An adopter running Agile SAFe already organizes work into epics, features, and user stories with a well-understood hierarchy of scope.
   A method that recognizes those terms as native kinds lets the adopter continue its existing planning cadence; a method that insists on substitute terms forces a translation layer that adds friction before any value is demonstrated.
