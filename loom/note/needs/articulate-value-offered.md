When an Organization or Platform Owner, or a Product Owner, has captured a need and the commitments that answer it, it wants a single authoritative statement, in the customer's own terms, of the relief or gain the product or service offers for that need, that everyone on the team, every customer-facing surface, and every explanation can draw from.

## Source

The value-proposition-design tradition, which sets an Offering (the relief and gains a product or service offers) against a customer profile (the persona's jobs, pains, and gains) and treats the fit between them as the thing to get right before building.

The need is sharpened by intent capture: once needs and obligations are first-class artifacts, the value that connects them has a natural home beside them rather than in a disconnected deck.

The need is reasoned from applying that tradition to first-class intent capture rather than observed in the field, the Offering and the Value Proposition being new to this corpus.

External to this corpus and not derived from it.
