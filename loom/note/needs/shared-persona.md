When the corpus records who a need is for, a Product Owner wants each actor named once and meaning the same person wherever it is referenced, an Organization or Platform Owner wants its teams to reuse, decline, or adapt the actors defined company-wide, and a Future Maintainer wants to follow any actor name to one definition and change it safely, so that an actor is not re-spelled and drifting across needs, a company-wide actor is reused rather than reinvented per team, and a later edit to who an actor is can be made with confidence.

## Source

The single-source-of-truth cost of restating an actor across artifacts, observed in this corpus where the persona field already near-duplicates and packs several actors into one string, and the established practice of treating an actor or stakeholder as a named, reusable entity (UML actors, ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148 stakeholder classes) rather than inline prose.

The same pull toward stating an actor once and letting a team hold a local variant, which shapes how shared obligations are handled across teams, applies here to personas.

External to this corpus and not derived from it.
