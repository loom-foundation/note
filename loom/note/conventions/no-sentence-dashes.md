id: note:conv:9y63hs5
name: No sentence dashes
kind: convention
status: current
---

Corpus prose does not use a dash to set a phrase off mid-sentence: neither an em-dash nor a spaced hyphen used as one.
Use a semicolon, a colon, parentheses, a comma, or a full stop instead.

The prohibition governs the dash as mid-sentence punctuation only.
The hyphen that joins a compound word (pain-reliever, gain-creator, single-source-of-truth) is correct and unaffected.
A dash standing alone in a table cell as a none or not-applicable marker is not setting a phrase off mid-sentence and is allowed; the em-dash is the conventional marker there.

The scope of this convention is the corpus's own prose artifacts and the methodology meta.
Files the repository only hosts and does not author are out of scope: the per-side LICENSE (the verbatim license text), LICENSE.md, NOTICE, PATENTS.md, CONTRIBUTING.md, and DCO governance files, and anything generated or git-ignored.
