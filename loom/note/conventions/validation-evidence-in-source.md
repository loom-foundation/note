When an artifact's [Validation](../terms/validation/validation.md){id=note:term:13esbh9} tier is raised, the evidence grounding the new tier is cited in the artifact's `## Source` section: an interview, an incident, usage data, or an experiment; for `established`, the external authority that settles it.
When a claim is recorded `invalidated`, the `## Source` cites what disproved it.

## Rationale

The validation tier is the corpus's honesty signal, separating an assumption from a confirmed finding; a tier raised with no cited evidence is an unbacked claim, and an author or agent under pressure to look thorough can inflate the dial until the signal is worthless.
Citing the evidence at the point of the raise keeps the tier trustworthy and makes an unbacked raise visible: a raised tier with an empty `## Source` is a flag.
