A reader shall be able to determine what a requirement obligates without ambiguity, and a requirement shall be expressed so that whether it is met is testable.

## Source

See the Source of [Unambiguous, testable requirements](../../needs/unambiguous-testable-requirements.md){id=note:need:e4k9w2n}.

## Rationale

An obligation that is read two ways is an obligation no one can rely on, and an obligation with no checkable criterion cannot be verified.

The requirement states the two qualities, unambiguous and testable, as properties required of every requirement;
how those qualities are achieved, a constrained phrasing or a quality check, is left to the system requirements and to specification, so the obligation commits to no convention.

The conditional case the need raises, an obligation that fires on an event, a state, or an unwanted situation, stating its trigger and response explicitly, is carried by that same constrained-phrasing requirement and the EARS profile it ships, not restated here.
