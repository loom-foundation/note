An adopter shall be able to bring an existing system under the method by capturing its intent incrementally and starting from what already exists, with recovered intent marked at honest confidence rather than omitted or overstated, without a blank-slate rewrite and without documenting the whole system up front before any value is gained.

## Source

See the Source of [Adopt the method on a system that already exists](../../needs/brownfield-adoption.md){id=note:need:b3k9w2t}.

## Rationale

Most systems adopting a method already exist;
a method usable only on greenfield work excludes them.

The obligation is that adoption can begin from a running system and proceed piece by piece, with recovered intent marked at honest confidence rather than omitted or overstated.

This rests on the method's existing maturity and validation vocabulary rather than a new confidence mechanism, and it leaves how a corpus is seeded from existing sources to the system requirements and to the tool.
