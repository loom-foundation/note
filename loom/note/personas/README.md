# Personas

The named actors the method's needs serve. Each persona file describes one actor and its stake; how the actors relate across the work lives here. These personas describe the method's primary setting, an engineering organization building with people and AI agents together, and generalize to any sustained body of work, whether a software system, a hardware product, a process, or a service.

The set is organized on four axes, not one: the activity an actor performs on the work, the scale at which they own it, the point in a corpus's life at which they arrive, and the kind of actor they are.

## By activity, across the weave

The work runs from intent to product: the Product Owner records the needs, the value, and what acceptable means; the Systems Engineer states the requirements and commits the specifications; the Implementer turns a specification into the built thing and cites the intent it realizes; the Verifier authors the check that confirms an obligation is met; and the Reviewer or Auditor governs and assures. Verification and governance are separate seats, and both are independent of whoever produced the work, because no one marks their own work.

The Systems Engineer is the primary persona: the practitioner who decides whether the method earns a trial on a real system.

## By scale of ownership

The Adopter, the Organization or Platform Owner, and the Technology Executive own adoption and its value at team, cross-team, and organizational scale; the Organization or Platform Owner folds in the platform and compliance owner. The value propositions are written above all to two readers: the Systems Engineer, who reads as a practitioner, and the Technology Executive, who reads as an investor in capability.

## By point in the corpus's life

The Future Maintainer and the Forking or Spun-Out Team are the actors a corpus meets later in its life, the one inheriting it in place and the other carrying it across a cut.

## By kind of actor

The AI Agent cuts across every role above: it is the machine-speed actor that may stand in any of them, the methodology-level agent, distinct from the tool-only agent personas a reference tool defines.

## Not personas here

- The end-user of an adopter's own product: a stakeholder of that product, not of the method.
- The maintainers of this corpus: the method's own project state, not an actor the method serves.
- Domain specialists such as a security engineer, a designer, or a site-reliability engineer: instances an Adopter casts in its own corpus.
- Tool builders and independent implementers: they build against the method from outside its boundary, served by its specifications rather than holding its needs.
- The Orchestrating Authority: the locus of identity and permission that owns verdicts and gates transitions, a system role the method answers to, not a need-holder it serves.
