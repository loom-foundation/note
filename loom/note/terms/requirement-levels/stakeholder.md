The level of an obligation as whoever holds the need experiences it, validated against the Needs.

A stakeholder is anyone whose need shapes the system, whether or not they ever use it; a board imposing a regulatory obligation is a stakeholder, not a user.

## Inclusion test

Is the obligation stated in the terms of whoever holds the need, validated against the Needs rather than against what is built?
If so, it is a stakeholder-level requirement; if instead the obligation is placed on the System and verified against what is built, that is the system level.

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "a person with an interest or concern in something"), generalized from its older sense of one who holds the stakes of a wager.

In requirements engineering the term names the parties whose needs a system must serve; ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148 distinguishes the stakeholder requirements specification, expressed in the stakeholders' terms and validated against their needs, from the system requirements derived from it.

This corpus widens the everyday "user" reading on purpose: a stakeholder is anyone whose need shapes the system, whether or not they ever operate it.
