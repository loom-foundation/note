The position of a Requirement on the stakeholder-to-system refinement axis.

Authoritative in the artifact's frontmatter; the directory layout is a derived convenience, and the identifier deliberately omits it so that re-leveling never breaks a reference.

## Inclusion test

Does the label answer "how far along the stakeholder-to-system refinement is this obligation?", placing it as a stakeholder-held want or a system-borne obligation, rather than what the artifact is (its kind), what quality it must exhibit (its type), or what it is about (its content)?
If so, it is a level.

## Relations

- *contrasts with* [Type](../requirement-typing/type.md){id=note:term:mwwzghp} (the quality axis of a Requirement: functional, non-functional, constraint)

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "a position on a scale of amount, quantity, extent, or quality").
In requirements engineering the stakeholder-to-system gradient is the long-standing distinction between stakeholder (or user) requirements and system requirements, codified in ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148, which separates the stakeholder requirements specification from the system requirements specification.

"Level" is chosen over "tier" or "layer" because it names a position on a single refinement axis rather than a stack of discrete strata.
