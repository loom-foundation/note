An authored proposal that was considered and deliberately declined, kept as a record rather than deleted, having never reached current.

It is terminal and off the maturity ladder; do not cite it as operative, only to reconstruct what was proposed and turned down.

## Inclusion test

Was an authored proposal weighed and deliberately declined, with the conclusion to decline worth keeping as a record?

If so, it is rejected; if the content was operative before being retired, that is obsolete, and if a draft was merely abandoned with no conclusion reached, it is deleted rather than recorded.

## Origin

The Status value-vocabulary, naming the off-ladder terminal state of an authored proposal that the organization's judgment tested and declined, kept as a record rather than deleted.
It is the lifecycle analogue of Validation's Invalidated, which is the off-ladder terminal tier for a claim that validation has disproven; where Invalidated records a claim that did not hold, Rejected records a proposal that was not adopted.
