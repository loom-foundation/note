A part of a System that is not decomposed further in the current context.

In this project's instantiation, the built-in packs are Components of Note: each is addressed as a unit at this level, and none stands alone apart from the substrate it extends.

## Inclusion test

Is it a constituent part addressed as a unit at the current level, without independent standalone coherence being claimed for it here?
If yes, it is a Component;
if instead it is coherent on its own terms while still being part of something larger, that is a Subsystem.

## Relations

- *is part of* [System](system.md){id=note:term:0jbwfkm}
- *contrasts with* [Subsystem](subsystem.md){id=note:term:mw1th2k} (a Subsystem is coherent in isolation, a Component need not be)

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "a part or element of a larger whole, especially a part of a machine or vehicle").

Standard in systems engineering through the INCOSE tradition, where a component is an element of a system addressed at a given level of decomposition, the level at which the system is treated as a black box rather than decomposed further.
