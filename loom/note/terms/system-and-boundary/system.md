An arrangement of parts that together achieve a coherent purpose and can be reasoned about on its own terms.

Note is a System: it has internal structure (a substrate, packs, and the artifacts riding them), its own vocabulary, and a purpose that stands without reference to anything outside it; Sett, the reference tool that reads and computes over Note corpora, is a System of its own.

When the discovery pack is enabled, the System is also an authorable kind: the system-of-interest that `provides` an offering's value, the Value Proposition Canvas's Products and Services made first-class, and the offering-side counterpart to the Job a Pain or Gain is `framedBy`.
A corpus with one system-of-interest keeps it implicit in its single Context; a `system` artifact is authored only when more than one must be told apart (a service per repository, several products in one corpus), and its versions are tagged snapshots rather than a kind of their own.

## Inclusion test

Does the thing have internal structure and stand as a coherent whole on its own terms?
If yes, it is a System (standalone) or a Subsystem (when embedded);
if instead it is an indivisible part with no independent coherence claimed for it here, that is a Component.

## Relations

- *relates to* [Orchestrating Authority](orchestrating-authority.md){id=note:term:80049zb} (may be embedded in; when embedded, the System is a Subsystem of the larger enclosing system)
- *relates to* [Offering](../artifact-kinds/offering.md){id=note:term:n5vn3ke} (the system-of-interest provides an offering's value; a `system` artifact carries the `provides` edge to the offerings, value-propositions, relievers, or creators it delivers, inverse `providedBy`)

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "a set of things working together as parts of a mechanism or an interconnected network").

Standard in systems engineering through the INCOSE tradition, where a system is an arrangement of elements achieving a purpose, and the system-of-interest is relative to the observer's boundary.
