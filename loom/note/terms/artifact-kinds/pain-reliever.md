A Pain Reliever is an offering that eliminates, reduces, or minimizes a specific Pain.

It is a facet of an Offering, stating what the product offers, in the customer's terms, benefit-led.

## Inclusion test

Does it state what the product offers to relieve a pain (a Pain Reliever), as opposed to the Pain itself, which the persona suffers?

A Pain Reliever must relieve a real Pain, not restate, in negative voice, a Gain a Gain Creator already produces.
Where a Reliever and a Creator would describe the same effect in opposite voice, only one has earned its place ([Earned Substance](../../principles/earned-substance.md){id=note:principle:yecemfe}); keep both only where the relief offered and the gain created genuinely differ.

## Relations

- *is part of* [Offering](offering.md){id=note:term:n5vn3ke}
- *relieves* [Pain](pain.md){id=note:term:hb74nmn}

## Origin

It is the value-map side of the Value Proposition Canvas (Osterwalder, Pigneur, Bernarda, and Smith, *Value Proposition Design*, 2014), which sets a product's pain relievers and gain creators against a persona's pains and gains.

The agentive distinction is what keeps it apart from the Pain it answers: a reliever produces the relief of a pain, naming what the product does, where the Pain names what the persona suffers.
