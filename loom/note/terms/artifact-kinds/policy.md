A mandated direction an organization sets for itself: a signed, owned instrument that mandates a set of obligations and is in force from a stated date until superseded, whose load-bearing fact is that the instrument itself is in force, not that the system does any single thing.

A Policy is the document of record an auditor points at, distinct from the obligations it mandates, which are Requirements beneath it.

## Inclusion test

Is it a signed, dated instrument that mandates a set of obligations, carried by the fact that the instrument is in force?

If it is a single solution-free obligation on the system, it is a Requirement; if it is a weighted value balanced against others, it is a Principle; if it names the compulsory mechanism that meets it, it is a Standard; if it is the ordered steps that carry it out, it is a Procedure.

## Relations

- *contrasts with* [Requirement](requirement.md){id=note:term:tr9zna6} (an instrument of record that mandates a set, versus a single solution-free obligation on the system)
- *contrasts with* [Principle](principle.md){id=note:term:q2snn7e} (a binding mandated direction in force, versus a weighted value balanced against others)
- *contrasts with* [Standard](standard.md){id=note:term:mgbbav4} (the mandated direction in force, versus the compulsory mechanism that makes it concrete)
- *contrasts with* [Procedure](procedure.md){id=note:term:9kb7n6y} (the mandated direction in force, versus the ordered steps that carry it out)

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "a course or principle of action adopted or proposed by a government, party, business, or individual").

In governance practice the sense is the top tier of the policy, standard, procedure hierarchy used across ISO/IEC 27001, NIST SP 800-12 and SP 800-53, ISACA, and the (ISC)² body of knowledge: a mandatory, management-approved statement of direction, distinct from the standards and procedures that implement it and from the controls it answers.
