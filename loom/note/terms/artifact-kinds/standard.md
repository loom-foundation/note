A compulsory mechanism, format, or threshold an organization imposes on itself to make a Policy concrete: a binding way of building or doing, named because the named mechanism is its substance, derived from the Policy it implements and conformed to by many systems.

A Standard is self-imposed: where an external force imposes the mechanism, the obligation is a constraint-typed Requirement carrying that force as its source, not a Standard.

## Inclusion test

Is it a self-imposed, compulsory mechanism the organization mandates of itself, standing above the obligations that conform to it and binding the system rather than the making of artifacts?

If an external force imposes the mechanism, it is a constraint-typed Requirement crosswalked to that force; if it commits a design that satisfies one requirement, it is a Specification; if it binds the making of artifacts by agreement rather than the system, it is a Convention; if it states the direction the standard makes concrete, it is the Policy above it.

## Relations

- *contrasts with* [Requirement](requirement.md){id=note:term:tr9zna6} (a self-imposed compulsory mechanism above many obligations, versus a single solution-free obligation that names no mechanism absent an external force)
- *contrasts with* [Specification](specification.md){id=note:term:9xd6ejd} (an organization-wide internal authority above many obligations, versus a committed design below the one requirement it satisfies)
- *contrasts with* [Convention](convention.md){id=note:term:avgac93} (a compulsory rule binding the system, versus an agreed rule binding the making of artifacts)

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "a level of quality or attainment").

In governance practice the sense is the middle tier of the policy, standard, procedure hierarchy (ISACA, SANS, the (ISC)² body of knowledge), and NIST SP 800-12's organizational standard, "normally compulsory within an organization," distinct from the publications of external standards bodies, which an adopter answers to through a crosswalk rather than authoring as its own.
