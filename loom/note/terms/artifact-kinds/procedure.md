The ordered, role-assigned steps that carry a Policy or Standard out when a trigger fires: an operational instrument, owned by the role that runs it and revised as practice changes, whose steps effect a change in the world rather than render a verdict.

A Procedure is a thing done, distinct from a Verification, which is a thing checked.

## Inclusion test

Is it an ordered sequence of steps, each performed by a named role and initiated by a trigger, that effects a change in the world?

If its steps render a pass-or-fail verdict, name a verification method, or carry an expected result, it is a Verification; if it commits a design rather than the steps to run it, it is a Specification; if it states the direction or the mechanism the steps carry out, it is a Policy or a Standard above it.

## Relations

- *contrasts with* [Verification](verification.md){id=note:term:6wmj8e9} (ordered steps that effect a state change and hold no verdict, versus a check that renders one)
- *contrasts with* [Specification](specification.md){id=note:term:9xd6ejd} (the role-assigned steps to perform when a trigger fires, versus the committed design they may carry out)
- *contrasts with* [Standard](standard.md){id=note:term:mgbbav4} (the ordered steps that carry an instrument out, versus the compulsory mechanism it makes concrete)

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "an established or official way of doing something").

In quality and service-management practice the sense is fixed by ISO 9001, ITIL, and COBIT as the step-level instrument below a process: who does what, in what order, when. The coarser cross-functional flow above it, the process, is held as a later addition to this pack rather than carried in this version.
