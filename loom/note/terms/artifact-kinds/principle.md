A named, weighted commitment that guides judgment across the corpus and grounds a class of obligations: a value that is balanced against other principles rather than applied all-or-nothing, adopted because it mitigates a risk or realizes a gain the corpus cares about.

It is the single home for a recurring rationale, cited by name and by id, that requirements, specifications, and decisions align with and enact.

A principle may carry an optional `altitude` recording the level at which it operates and the reader it serves, one of value, operating, or authoring ([Altitude](../principle-altitudes/altitude.md){id=note:term:a1h1jre}); a held good of who the authors are (a "value") is recorded as a principle at the value altitude, not as a separate kind.

## Inclusion test

Is it a weighted value that is balanced against others, the standing reason a class of obligations follows, rather than a single statement that is satisfied or violated with no middle?

If it is true-or-false as a unit, it is a Requirement or an authoring rule; if it states a persona's problem, it is a Need; if it records a single choice and its rationale, it is a Decision.

A Principle is the positive rule those others rest on.

## Origin

Oxford: "a fundamental truth or proposition that serves as the foundation for a system of belief or behavior, or for a chain of reasoning."

The principles-versus-rules distinction is Dworkin's: a rule applies in an all-or-nothing fashion, while a principle has weight and is balanced against others, which fixes a Principle here as the weighted, balanced kind.

A held good ranked by priority is a Value in the sense of Rokeach and Schwartz; Note records such a value as a principle at the `value` altitude rather than a separate kind, since it carries no inbound relation a principle lacks ([Principle altitude field](../../decisions/principle-altitude-field.md){id=note:dec:3vg9jdh}).

The architecture-principle template, Name, Statement, Rationale, Implications, is TOGAF's; the management-system sense, a foundational commitment a system is built to honor, is ISO 9000's quality-management principles and ISO 31000's risk principles.
