A task a persona is trying to get done, functional, social, or emotional.

A Job is the framing facet of a Need, the thing against which its Pains and Gains are felt; a Pain or Gain encodes that framing with the `framedBy` edge, whose derived inverse `frames` reads from the Job, and which a facet authors on itself, never the Job (the inverse is never authored).

## Inclusion test

Does it name what the persona is trying to do, stated independently of any mechanism for doing it?

If it names a mechanism the solution must provide, it is a Requirement; if it names a means the product offers, it is a pain-reliever or gain-creator; a Job stays at the level of the task itself, the outcome the persona is reaching for, not how it is reached.

## Relations

- *is part of* [Need](need.md){id=note:term:x4vqdj1}

## Origin

The jobs-to-be-done tradition: the marketing adage that a customer wants the hole, not the drill (popularized by Levitt); Ulwick's outcome-driven innovation, which frames the job as a stable, solution-free desired outcome; and Christensen's "job to be done", a situation a person is trying to make progress in.

Sharpened by the customer profile of the Value Proposition Canvas (Osterwalder et al.), which casts customer jobs (functional, social, and emotional) as the frame a persona's pains and gains are felt against.
