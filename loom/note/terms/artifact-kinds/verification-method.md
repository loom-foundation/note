The means by which a Verification's check is carried out: one of test, analysis, inspection, demonstration, or formal proof.

Authoritative in the Verification's frontmatter; it fixes how the criteria are exercised, not whether the run passed, which the Orchestrating Authority owns.

## Inclusion test

Does the label answer "by what means is the check carried out?", naming test, analysis, inspection, demonstration, or formal proof, rather than what is checked or whether it passed?

If so, it is a verification method.

## Origin

ISO/IEC/IEEE 29148 and the INCOSE verification tradition, which enumerate the standard verification methods (test, analysis, inspection, demonstration), with formal proof added where a mathematical argument is the means of assurance.
