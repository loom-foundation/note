The classification axis of a Pain: which of three structurally distinct kinds of negative experience it is.

Authoritative in the `subkind` frontmatter field on a Pain artifact; optional and available at every profile ([Pain subkind field](../../decisions/pain-subkind-field.md){id=note:dec:qc2nc57}).

Its value is one of the three sub-kinds below.

## Inclusion test

Does the label answer "what kind of pain is this: something already endured, something blocking the Job, or something feared but not yet realized?", rather than what the Pain is about (its content) or how validated the claim is (Validation)?
If it classifies the structural character of the negative experience, it is the Subkind axis.

## Origin

General English ("subkind": a subdivision of a kind or category).
The three sub-kinds follow the Value Proposition Canvas pain classification (Osterwalder et al.), which distinguishes between functional problems, obstacles, and risks the persona carries; the compound "subkind" is coined here because the Term's own vocabulary calls them "sub-kinds" and no shorter established term names the axis ([Pain subkind field](../../decisions/pain-subkind-field.md){id=note:dec:qc2nc57}).
