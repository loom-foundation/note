A potential, not-yet-realized undesired outcome the persona fears: something bad that could happen, as opposed to something already happening or something blocking the Job today.

## Inclusion test

Is the pain something the persona fears might occur in the future, rather than an outcome they already endure or a barrier that blocks the Job right now?
If it is potential and unrealized, it is a Risk; if the persona endures it now, it is a Problem; if it blocks the Job itself, it is an Obstacle.

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "a situation involving exposure to danger; the possibility that something unpleasant or unwelcome will happen").
The Value Proposition Canvas (Osterwalder et al.) classifies risks as the third pain category: potential negative outcomes the customer fears, which motivate avoidance behavior even before the harm is realized.
The distinction between an already-endured problem and a feared potential outcome is well-established in risk management (ISO 31000), where risk is "the effect of uncertainty on objectives" and specifically concerns potential, not actualized, outcomes.
