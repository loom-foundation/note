A day-to-day commitment that guides how work is done: a principle that turns a Value into behavior and decisions, the practical level at which most commitments live.

## Inclusion test

Does the principle direct everyday practice and decision-making, how we work, rather than naming bedrock identity (Value) or governing how the corpus itself is written (Authoring)?
If it turns a value into daily conduct, its altitude is Operating.

A commitment that has hardened into a signed, dated mandate is no longer a principle but a Policy (with its measurable criteria as Standards and its steps as Procedures), carried by the governance pack.

## Relations

- *is a value of* [Altitude](altitude.md){id=note:term:a1h1jre}

## Origin

General English ("operating": relating to the routine functioning and conduct of an activity).

"Operating principles" is established usage in organizational and engineering practice for the practical commitments that direct day-to-day conduct, distinct from the identity they express; Ray Dalio's *Principles* (2017) and the Amazon Leadership Principles are worked instances of the form.
The boundary with the governance kinds is ratification: a weighed preference is a Principle; a ratified, dated mandate is a Policy ([Governance as an opt-in pack](../../decisions/governance-pack.md){id=note:dec:59ptf2z}).
