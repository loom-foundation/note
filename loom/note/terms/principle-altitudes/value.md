A bedrock commitment naming a good the corpus holds as desirable in itself: who its authors are, the standing reason the operating and authoring principles serve, prescribing no single practice of its own.

A value is recorded as a Principle at the `value` altitude, not as a separate kind: it is a weighted commitment like any principle, distinguished only by operating at the identity level ([Principle altitude field](../../decisions/principle-altitude-field.md){id=note:dec:3vg9jdh}).
The token is distinct from the Value Proposition and the value map of the discovery pack, which concern the customer value an offering delivers, not a held principle.

## Inclusion test

Is the principle a bedrock statement of who we are, a good held desirable in itself that other principles serve and draw from, rather than a commitment about how work is done day to day (Operating) or how the corpus itself is written (Authoring)?
If it is the identity the others rest on, its altitude is Value.

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "principles or standards of behavior; one's judgement of what is important in life"): the term names a held good, what is judged important, not a procedure for pursuing it.

In the study of values this is the trans-situational held good of Rokeach (*The Nature of Human Values*, 1973) and Schwartz (*Theory of Basic Human Values*, 1992): an enduring belief, ordered by priority, that an end-state or mode of conduct is desirable.
The values-and-principles split is the Agile Manifesto's (four values ranked by "over"; twelve principles that operationalize them) and the architecture-principle tradition's (TOGAF, ISO 9000).
Note does not make Value a separate kind: a value an obligation aligns with is a weighted commitment balanced against others, which is a Principle; the `value` altitude records that the principle sits at the identity level, the deepest "why" the others serve ([Principle altitude field](../../decisions/principle-altitude-field.md){id=note:dec:3vg9jdh}).
