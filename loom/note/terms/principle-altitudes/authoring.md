A meta-level commitment governing how the corpus itself is written: a principle about the craft of the artifacts, not about the work they describe.

## Inclusion test

Does the principle govern how intent is authored and maintained, the corpus as an artifact, rather than naming identity (Value) or guiding the work itself (Operating)?
If its subject is the making and keeping of the corpus, its altitude is Authoring.

An authoring Principle is the weighted value (for example Single Source of Truth); the concrete, arbitrary practice that puts it into effect (a casing scheme, a file layout) is a Convention that `enacts` it, not an authoring Principle.

## Origin

General English ("authoring": the action of writing or originating a work).

The distinction between a principle about the product and a principle about the corpus mirrors the method's own separation of intent from the Convention rules that bind its authoring.
The principle-versus-convention boundary is Lewis's (*Convention: A Philosophical Study*, 1969): the weighted value is the Principle, the arbitrary-among-equals practice that enacts it is the Convention.
