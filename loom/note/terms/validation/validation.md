The grading axis for how validated a claim is: the strength of confirmation behind a Need, a Pain or Gain, or a Value Proposition, recorded so an unvalidated assumption can be told from a confirmed finding and raised as it is validated.

Optional, carried in the `validation` frontmatter field; its value is one of the five tiers.
The evidence behind each raise is cited in the artifact's `## Source` ([Validation evidence in the Source](../../conventions/validation-evidence-in-source.md){id=note:conv:8wafpx1}).

## Inclusion test

Does the label answer "how validated is this claim?", grading the confirmation behind it, rather than how mature the artifact is (Status) or whether it is well-formed (Validation State)?
If it grades the strength of validation, it is the validation axis.

## Relations

- *contrasts with* [Status](../lifecycle/status.md){id=note:term:xcen4v2} (validation strength, not artifact maturity)
- *contrasts with* [Validation State](../conformance-and-evolution/validation-state.md){id=note:term:fjmzfre} (how validated a claim is, not the result of a well-formedness check)

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "the action of checking or proving the validity or accuracy of something").

The tiering of confirmation strength follows evidence-grading traditions such as GRADE in evidence-based practice, and the validate-or-retire loop of lean and customer-development practice, where an assumption is held explicitly until it is tested.
