A claim settled and externally verifiable, not in serious dispute: a regulation, a standard, a replicated finding, a property of the substrate.

## Inclusion test

Could a competent outsider confirm it against an authority without trusting us?
If so, the claim is Established; if it can only be confirmed by retracing our own reasoning or recalling what we saw, it is Inferred or Observed instead.

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "established" as "accepted or recognized as true; not in dispute").

In this corpus's validation tiering, Established names the settled status of a claim in the world, the strongest of the five tiers.
