The `validation` tier records how validated a claim is, and is marked honestly: an unvalidated assumption is allowed, but it must say so rather than be dressed as confirmed. The field is optional, and it may sit on a Need, a Pain or Gain facet, or a Value Proposition; it grades one embedded claim, that the problem is real and the persona has it, or that the offered value lands, not the want itself, since a want is not true or false.

The five tiers share a strength ordering but measure two different things. Assumed, Inferred, and Observed describe how confirmed a claim is: guessed, reasoned, or directly witnessed. Established describes the settled status of the claim in the world, independent of how we came to it; it applies only when the claim is externally settled, a regulatory mandate being the clean case, which is rare, so most claims are Assumed, Inferred, or Observed. Invalidated is off that ladder: a claim that validation has tested and disproven, kept as a record rather than deleted. Validation is distinct from Validation State, which is the result of a well-formedness check (valid, invalid, or unresolved).
