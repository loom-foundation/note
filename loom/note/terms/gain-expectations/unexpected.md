A gain the persona did not anticipate, would not have known to ask for, and is delighted by when encountered: an outcome that creates excitement or positive surprise precisely because it was outside the persona's expectation space.

## Inclusion test

Would the persona not have listed this outcome if asked what they want from the Job, yet would respond with delight when they experience it?
If it is unanticipated and delightful, it is Unexpected; if the persona would have named it as something they hope for, it is Desired; if it is the standard norm, it is Expected; if its absence is a failure, it is Required.

## Relations

- *is a value of* [Expectation](expectation.md){id=note:term:na2bqej}

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "not expected or regarded as likely to happen").
Kano attractive quality (Kano et al., 1984): the category of outcomes that delight the customer without being explicitly requested; their presence produces disproportionate satisfaction, but their absence is not noticed since the customer had no expectation of them.
The Value Proposition Canvas classifies this level as "unexpected gains": outcomes beyond even the customer's desires and expectations, which they discover they value only after encountering them.
