A gain the persona treats as a non-negotiable floor: its absence is felt as a pain, not merely as a missed benefit, and no other quality compensates for it.

## Inclusion test

Would the persona be dissatisfied, or refuse adoption, if this outcome were absent, even if every other outcome were excellent?
If the absence is itself painful, it is Required; if its absence is merely disappointing, or if the persona could manage without it, it sits higher on the ladder as Expected, Desired, or Unexpected.

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "officially compulsory; or otherwise essential").
Kano must-be quality (Kano et al., 1984): the category of outcomes whose absence causes dissatisfaction but whose presence is taken for granted and earns no extra satisfaction.
The Value Proposition Canvas gain classification aligns this level with "required gains": the minimum outcomes the customer needs for a solution to be considered at all.
