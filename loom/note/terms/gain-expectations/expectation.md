The position of a Gain on the Kano expectation ladder: how strongly the persona holds the outcome as a floor, a norm, a preference, or a surprise.

Authoritative in the `expectation` frontmatter field on a Gain artifact; optional and available at every profile ([Gain expectation field](../../decisions/gain-expectation-field.md){id=note:dec:c1m3hpn}).

Its value is one of the four levels below.

## Inclusion test

Does the label answer "how strongly does the persona expect or value this outcome: as an absolute requirement, a standard norm, a wanted extra, or an unanticipated delight?", rather than what the Gain is about (its content) or how validated the claim is (Validation)?
If it places the gain on the expectation ladder, it is the Expectation axis.

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "a belief that something will happen or is likely to happen").
The Kano model (Kano et al., 1984) grades desired outcomes by how strongly customers hold them: from must-be requirements through one-dimensional expectations to attractive qualities; that grading is exactly the expectation ladder this term names.
The Value Proposition Canvas (Osterwalder, Pigneur, Bernarda, and Smith, 2014) reinforces the same four-tier distinction in its gain-level classification.
