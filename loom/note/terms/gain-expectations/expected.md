A gain the persona holds as the standard outcome in their mental model of how the Job gets done: better delivery satisfies; worse delivery dissatisfies, on a roughly linear scale.

## Inclusion test

Would the persona experience more or less satisfaction in direct proportion to how well or poorly this outcome is delivered, rather than treating its absence as an absolute failure or treating it as a pleasant surprise?
If satisfaction tracks delivery on a one-dimensional scale, it is Expected; if its absence is an absolute floor it is Required; if getting it would be a welcome extra rather than a norm, it is Desired or Unexpected.

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "regarded as likely; anticipated").
Kano one-dimensional quality (Kano et al., 1984): the category of outcomes where customer satisfaction increases proportionally with fulfillment; these are the stated, explicit requirements a persona brings to a job.
The Value Proposition Canvas classifies this level as "expected gains": outcomes the customer takes for granted as part of the standard offer.
