A gain the persona wants and would value beyond the expected level, but does not hold as a floor: a positive outcome they hope for, know is possible, and would appreciate, without requiring it.

## Inclusion test

Would the persona welcome this outcome and consider it a positive differentiator, while not being dissatisfied if it were absent, since they know it goes beyond the standard?
If it is a wanted extra beyond the norm, it is Desired; if its absence is painful, it is Required; if it is a routine standard, it is Expected; if the persona had not anticipated it at all before encountering it, it is Unexpected.

## Relations

- *is a value of* [Expectation](expectation.md){id=note:term:na2bqej}

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "strongly wished for or intended").
The Kano model does not have a single named category for this tier; it corresponds to the upper range of one-dimensional quality and the territory approaching attractive quality, where the customer explicitly wants but does not require the outcome.
The Value Proposition Canvas classifies this level as "desired gains": outcomes the customer would like but that go beyond what they currently expect.
