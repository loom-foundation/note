The quality axis of a Requirement: whether it states a behavior the system performs, a quality it exhibits, or a restriction on the solution space.

Authoritative in the artifact's frontmatter; it gates the solution-free rule, since a Constraint may name a mechanism that an external force imposes while a Functional or Non-functional requirement may not.

## Inclusion test

Does the label answer "what kind of obligation is this: a behavior, a quality, or a restriction?", rather than what the artifact is (its Kind), how far the stakeholder-to-system refinement has gone (its Level), or what it is about (its content)?
If so, it is a type.

## Origin

General English (Oxford: "a category of people or things having common characteristics").

The functional / non-functional split is standard in requirements practice (ISO/IEC 25010 names the non-functional quality attributes), and the Constraint type follows INCOSE usage, where a constraint bounds the design rather than describing behavior.

"Type" names the requirement-quality axis here, kept distinct from Kind, which classifies the artifact itself.
